Korean - Consonant

ㄱ(Giyok)

The first letter of Hangul. It is called the base station, and it is used as a beginning, as in 'A', and as a longitudinal, as in 'station'. I blocked my crotch with my udder and raised my tongue rope. It is silent when primordial, voiced between vowels, and pronounced without removing tongue roots when it is vertical. The tongue is the letter made to imitate the shape of the throat.

ㄴ(Nieun)

The second letter of the Korean alphabet. It is called 'nin', and it is used as a beginning, as in 'I', and as a longitudinal, as in 'line'. It is a voicing that causes resonance of the nasal cavity by putting the tip of the tongue on the alveoli and releasing it to expose the nasal cavity to the nostrils. When it is a bell-tongue, pronounce it without lifting the tip of the tongue. The tip of the tongue is the letter made to mimic the shape attached to the alveoli.

ㄷ(Digeut)

The third letter of the Korean alphabet. It is called 'Diet', and it is used as a beginning as in 'Dahn', as in '귿'. It is a plosive sound when it blocks the crotch with the umbilical cord and puts the tip of the tongue on the alveolar bone to stop the mouth and pops it. When it is a bell, it is pronounced without breaking it at the end. The shape of the tongue that makes this sound is like 'ㄴ', but it is a letter made by adding one stroke because it is pronounced hard.

ㄹ(Rieul)

The fourth letter of Hangul. It is called 'Lee', and it is used as a beginning as in 'La', and as a longitudinal in 'Ten'. Among the vowels, the tip of the tongue is a sound that vibrates while tapping the tongue once, and when it is a bongchung, it attaches the tip of the tongue to the alveoli and sends air to both sides of the tongue.

ㅁ(Mieum)

The fifth letter of Korean alphabet. It is called 'Miseum', and it is used as a beginning, as in 'Ma', as in 'Night'. It is a meteoric rhythm that keeps your mouth shut and exhales into your nose and hears your voice. When it is a bell, I pronounce it without lifting my lips. It is a letter made by mimicking the shape of two lips facing each other when making sound.

Korean - Vowel

ㅏ(Ah)

The fifteenth letter of the Korean alphabet. It used similar with 'Oh,' and 'ga, ga'.

ㅓ(Uh)

The seventeenth letter of the Korean alphabet. It is called 'uh', and it is used as neutral as in 'ke, ke'.
